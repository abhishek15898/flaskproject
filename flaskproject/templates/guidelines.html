  2. There should be minimum of 7000 words in the project report.
  3. Line Height: 1.5
  4. Headings Font size: 16px
  5. Sub-headings Font size: 14px
  6. Paragraphs: 12px
  7. Font-Family: Times New Roman(Preferred)
  8. Ensure that captions for images should be below the image, with numbering like fig 1.7. where "1" indicates chapter number - 1 and "7" indicates 7th figure of chapter 1.
  9. Ensure that captions for tables should be above the table. (Similar to Images)

Chapter 1: Introduction

Include the broad area and scope of your project. Specify where is your project going to be used and which sectors will be benefited by your project. Also specify what was your aim and motivation behind building your project. You may also consider specifying the tools and frameworks, in general and brief, that you will be using in this project in about 1-2 paragraphs. Also ensure to include a rough figure/layout of how your platform/ website/idea works.

Remember: This is a general introduction chapter, and you are NOT expected to specify technical terms and functionalities here. Introductory part is similar to explaining your project to a layman or a non-technical person. Hence, don't go into muct details.

To summerize:
This section answers the following questions:
 1. Where is your project used?
 2. Which sector will be benefited with your project?
 3. You aim and motivation?

Chapter 2: Requirement Analysis/Specification Analysis

In this chapter, you are required to specify steps initiated by you and your team to gather further information about the user requirements. These include data collection procedures, functional and non-functional requirements. You should include Use-Case diagrams. Every usecase and its associated actor(s) must be described elaborately. ER Diagram is an added advantage for Specification Analysis.

Remember: You are collecting information from the user/client. Hence, ensure to limit the use of technical terms here and map the user- requirements in the form of diagrams.

To summerize:
  1. Maximize the use of diagrams.
  2. Diagrams include: Use case Diagrams and ER Diagrams.
  3. Everything is as per user/client point of view.

Go to Index

Chapter 3: Implementation

This chapter takes in view of a programmer or a software developer. You need to specify every corner cases that can occur in your project, so that the software developer can take in mind those steps before writing the code. These cases could be from the Normal cases to the Exceptional Cases. If your project requires special algorithms/technology (such as any Machine Learning Algo/Special Sorting and Searching Algorithms/ or classifiers), then do specify them.

Remember: The project hasn't yet started and just resides in your brain! You are now mapping the requirements with a development environment. Hence, ensure to specify every single scenario with the use of Sequence Diagrams.

To summerize:
  1. Think in terms of a Software Developer.
  2. Diagrams to be included: Sequence Diagrams.
  3. Specify algorithms/libraries/modules you would use in this project.

Go to Index

Chapter 4: Language Implementation and Results

This is where you have started implementation (or coding) for your project. You may include the tools, framworks, language etc resources you have used for building this project. It is expected that you also include sample code structures or pseudocodes (whereever possible) to help other developers (so called co-developers) understand your code structure. If your project comes with a backend implementation where databases are used, then also include SQL queries.

Remember: The project has started. We are including this chapter to let other developers understand how far the project has reached (or implementated.) A new developer in your project may refer to this chapter to understand the work flow till now and would remain updated.

To summerize:
  1. Think in terms of a new developer who has just joined your project.
  2. Stuffs to include: File structure/directory, SQL Queries and References (wherever required)
  3. Software/Project Setup Instructions to be included (if any).

Go to Index

Chapter 5: Test Cases - Results

In this chapter, you are expected to showcase every implemented scenario or test case, which were described in Chapter 3. Include code samples, screenshots and images help better understand each implemented scenario. Also execute testing methodologies such as Black-Box testing, Grey-Box testing etc., incase you have tested your product.

Remember: The project is being developed (or ready). You need to showcase how validate are your functions/components in the project with the help of screenshots and simulations.

To summerize:
  1. Think in terms of a Software Tester. Test your products at its core limits!
  2. Stuffs to include: Screenshots, Simulations and Test Results Output.
  3. You may also include tools for testing.

Go to Index

Chapter 6: Conclusion

This is a project-specific section and may not be generalised for all the projects. However, ideas for conclusion may include your motivation to begin this project, tools and technologies used, implementations, your focus to further scale your project etc.

Remember: Be Specific. (Preferably explain in 2-3 paragraphs)

To summerize:
  1. Explain how you could further scale your project.
  2. Stuffs to include: Preferably Nothing.
